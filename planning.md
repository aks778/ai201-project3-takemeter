# TakeMeter — Planning

## Community

[r/LetsTalkMusic](https://www.reddit.com/r/LetsTalkMusic/) is a subreddit for in-depth music discussion (it bans low-effort posts and self-promo). I chose it because the community is organized around the *quality of a take* — members prize "argue your point" over "I just like it." It's a good fit for classification because its discourse varies in form: a 30-post sample spanned evidence-backed arguments, bold unsupported claims, and pure emotional reactions, giving the model real distinctions to learn.

## Hard edge cases

Two boundaries cause real ambiguity:
- **`analysis` vs. `hot_take`** — sounds argued but isn't (e.g. #15). **Rule:** if specific verifiable evidence would support the claim even without the opinion framing → `analysis`; if support is hand-waving/decorative → `hot_take`.
- **`hot_take` vs. `reaction`** — an emotional post that smuggles in a claim (e.g. #2). **Rule:** general claim about the music → `hot_take`; only a personal feeling → `reaction`.

When truly 50/50, I default to the lower-evidence label (reaction > hot_take > analysis) to keep the `analysis` class clean.

## Data collection plan

- **Source:** Reddit's official API via PRAW, run locally (the harness can't reach Reddit; [PullPush](https://pullpush.io) is a stale fallback). Pull from `new`, `hot`, and `top`/`controversial`.
- **Target:** ~200 labeled examples, ~65 per label.
- **Over-collection:** ~40% of `new` is out-of-scope, so pull ~330 raw to net 200; filter out-of-scope at collection, no junk label.
- **If a label is short** (likely `analysis`): targeted backfill from `top`/most-discussed threads, and log how many came from backfill.

## Evaluation metrics

- **Macro-F1 (headline):** average of per-class F1, equally weighted
- **Per-class precision/recall:** especially recall on `analysis` (don't miss real arguments) and precision on `hot_take` (don't flag arguments as empty takes).
- **Confusion matrix:** confirm errors fall on the two expected boundaries

## Definition of success

1. **Beats a lazy guesser** — much higher macro-F1 than always predicting the common label.
2. **Solid headline** — macro-F1 ≥ ~0.70.
3. **Reliable across labels** — no class below ~0.60 F1, with strong recall on `analysis` and precision on `hot_take`.

Deployment bar: a **helper, not an autopilot** — it flags likely strong arguments vs. quick takes, but a human decides. Occasional borderline errors are fine; systematically missing good arguments is not.

2. **Annotation assistance (during):** Claude will pre-label each post and then I will review each one. 
3. **Failure analysis (after):** give the misclassified examples to an LLM to group into patterns (e.g. "reasoning-shaped but evidence-free → called `analysis`"). I verify each pattern against the actual posts and quantify it ("7 of 12 fit") before it goes in the writeup.
